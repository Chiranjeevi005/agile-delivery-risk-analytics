# Problem Statement: Sprint Delivery Predictability Failure

## Context
Agile teams rely on sprint commitments to deliver features predictably. However, many teams fail to complete committed work within sprint timelines, leading to systemic delivery delays.

## Core Problem
Teams lack early visibility into sprint delivery risk, resulting in:
* **Missed Deadlines**: Delayed feature releases affecting product roadmaps.
* **Frequent Spillover**: High volume of tasks carried over to subsequent sprints.
* **Reduced Stakeholder Trust**: Declining confidence from business units in engineering timelines.
* **Inefficient Resource Utilization**: Misalignment between team capacity and work throughput.

## Root Cause Hypothesis
* **Overcommitment**: Planning more work than historical velocity allows.
* **Monitoring Gaps**: Lack of mid-sprint visibility into progress-to-goal metrics.
* **Predictive Deficit**: Absence of early warning indicators for delivery bottlenecks.
* **WIP Bottlenecks**: Excessive Work-In-Progress (WIP) leading to context switching and stalled items.

## Business Impact
* **20–40% Spillover Rate**: Observed in low-performing teams, creating a massive backlog drag.
* **Unreliable Forecasts**: Inability to provide accurate quarterly or release planning forecasts.
* **Operational Costs**: Increased overhead due to constant rework, task carry-over, and administrative overhead.

## Final Problem Statement
Agile teams are unable to predict sprint delivery risk early, leading to frequent spillovers and unreliable delivery outcomes, which negatively impact planning accuracy and stakeholder confidence.
