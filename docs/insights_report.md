# Insight Report

# 1. Overview

This report analyzes sprint performance data to identify inefficiencies in delivery, quantify their impact, and provide actionable insights for improving sprint predictability.

---

## 2. Key Observed Patterns

### 2.1 Severe Overcommitment

- Planned Work (Sprint_01): 12 items
- Completed Work: 3 items
- Commitment Reliability: 0.25

Insight:
The team consistently commits to more work than it can realistically deliver.

---

### 2.2 High Delivery Risk

- Risk Classification: Critical — High Confidence
- Reliability is significantly below acceptable threshold (0.7)

Insight:
Current sprint planning approach introduces high execution risk.

---

### 2.3 Presence of Spillover

- Spillover (Sprint_01): 1 item
- Indicates incomplete work carried forward

Insight:
Unfinished work accumulates across sprints, reducing efficiency.

---

### 2.4 Unstable Velocity

- Velocity drops from 3 → 0 across sprints

Insight:
Delivery capacity is inconsistent and unreliable.

---

## 3. Root Cause Analysis

| Issue          | Root Cause                        |
| -------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Overcommitment | Lack of capacity-based planning   |
| Spillover      | No mid-sprint monitoring          |
| Low velocity   | Poor workload distribution        |
| High risk      | No early risk detection mechanism |

---

## 4. Risk Indicators

- Commitment Reliability < 0.5 → High Risk
- Spillover present → Execution inefficiency
- Velocity decline → Capacity instability

---

## 5. Summary Insight

Only 25% of planned work is delivered within sprint, indicating severe overcommitment, lack of execution discipline, and high delivery risk.
